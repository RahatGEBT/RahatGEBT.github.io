New Publication in Cancer Discovery!

Published: 2025

Our latest research, "Bone-Induced HER2 Promotes Secondary Metastasis in HR+/HER2- Breast Cancer," demonstrates that blocking HER2-mediated secondary metastasis improves clinical outcome and establishes HER2 as a critical biomarker for bone metastasis in patients with initial HR+/HER2- breast cancer, representing ∼70% of all cases. This work, published in the high-impact journal *Cancer Discovery*, addresses an urgent need for alternative strategies to halt metastasis progression.

Cancer Discovery (2025)

Bone-Induced HER2 Promotes Secondary Metastasis in HR+/HER2- Breast Cancer

Alam R, Reva A, Edwards DG, Lege BM, Munoz Arcos L, Reduzzi C, Singh S, Hao X, Wu YH, Tian Z, Natalee LM, Damle G, Demircioglu D, Wang Y, Wu L, Molteni E, Hasson D, Lim B, Gugala Z, Chipuk JE, Lang JE, Sparano JA, Cheng C, Cristofanilli M, Xiao H, Zhang XH, Bado IL.

This groundbreaking study identifies how bone-induced HER2 signaling drives secondary metastasis in HR+/HER2- breast cancer, a common and aggressive subtype. Our findings demonstrate that targeting this HER2-mediated pathway can significantly improve clinical outcomes. This work establishes HER2 as a crucial prognostic biomarker for bone metastasis in these patients, offering a novel therapeutic strategy to combat metastatic relapse and enhance patient survival.
